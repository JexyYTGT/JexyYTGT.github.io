Good Things Come To An End

Nothing is permanent, whether it's good or bad.

Almost everyone who grew from a child definitely doesn’t think much about his or her childhood as a daily activity. It’s underappreciated a lot. Many given things that almost every single living being gets underappreciated for what they have. I felt like I underappreciated my childhood when I was a child, and I want to share a good memory of my life that I still think about to this day.

This was during the early summer of 2021, and if I can recall, possibly on June 21st. My parents were throwing a Graduation party for my sister who has graduated from high school, and well, I’m sure you know that the usual graduation party is ours as well: a huge cake to be given to everyone. It was tasty, yes, but it was given at the near end of the event. What really happened was in the middle.

I recall me and my friends hanging out, eating the good typical father-made barbeque chicken outside. After we finished, I asked them if they wanted to play, "Futbol Possession.” It’s basically when someone has possession of the soccer ball, they try to keep it as much as they can before someone takes possession. Literally after like 2 minutes, me and a friend raced to get the rolling-away ball but he went a bit aggressive and pushed me a little. All soccer players tend to get aggressive, and well, couldn’t really blame him since I was purposely blocking him from trying to get ball possession. And so, I tripped. Hard. On the concrete sidewalk.

I remember my little self just laying there for about 3 seconds before getting up with a pained expression on my face. I used my right arm to push myself up, since my left arm was hurting way more than my right arm. I then started to cry, not only because of how much pain I was experiencing, but also because of how like 4 different people who weren’t even around where me and my friends were playing just suddenly came and rushed to help comfort me.

“Your lips are scratched up like crazy,” a guest around 17-18 at the time said.

I gradually calmed down from crying, and then the next thing I just wanted to do was go in the bathroom and try to wash off the sensation of my skin on the left arm of burning. I told everyone that I was okay and that I could handle myself, and almost everyone went back to their business, except the guest who told me that my lips were scratched helped my limping little me get inside my house.

I went to the bathroom, washed my hands, face, left arm and hand, and went back downstairs to the living room. I spotted 2 free seats on the couch, which is rare to see when you have 60+ guests at your house and backyard, so I took a seat.

Now, for the next 3-4 hours, I just sat on that spot on the couch. After about 20 minutes on the couch, barely moving, most of the pain was gone, but only my left arm stayed. My left arm was resting on a pillow, so I was happy that it felt soft and cold to try and ease the nerves in my body. Since I was around 10-11, and I should’ve played around like the average kid my age, I couldn’t really do anything but just watch kids play around, people walk around in and out of the house, kids screaming upstairs slamming my sister’s bedroom door, and young adults just having a conversation on what in the world happened to my left arm.

The next day, I visited the hospital and eventually realised I fractured my wrist. How, unique and extraordinary for a child to accomplish. I realised that a lot of things happen when you're not doing anything; rather you’re just sitting and watching whatever happens, happen. I thought to myself that responsibility would probably take away the childhood away from a child, and many more things that grown-ups encounter would learn how to from when they’re a child.
